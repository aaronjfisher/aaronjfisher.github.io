Don't go to Hogwarts if you're into science

Wed 12 March 2014
By Aaron Fisher

In Blog.

tags: humorsciencemagicblack-box models

I sometimes hear people say that with they could've gone to Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry (HSWW), but there’s a drawback of HSWW that's often overlooked. There are a lot of things you can do with a degree from HSWW, but the one thing that’s hard to do is to become a scientist. In fact, there's very little emphasis on science in the HSWW culture. Students learn plenty of spells and potion recipes, but nobody at the school has a good understanding of how magic actually works -- after thousands of years, they still call it magic! It’s possible that no one in the wizarding world has ever studied the mechanisms underlying magic, but I don't buy it. How could wizards have witnessed the muggle scientific revolution, and looked at wands under a microscope? No, there must be scientific research institutions in the wizarding world too, which leads to only conclusion:

Hogwarts is a technical school. It's where you go to learn basic life skills, and maybe a craft. It’s great at sports, but not so great with cutting edge research.

Of course, HSWW was on the front lines of preventing a global holocaust, while professors from Beauxbatons and Durmstrang Institute were off in their ivory towers, presumably debating time travel paradoxes, or developing new languages for object-oriented spell casting. When shit hit the fan, HSWW stepped up.

There must be another school out there though that’s experimenting with new yeast strains for butter-beer, and running clinical trials on healing charms. Day to day, that’s the place to be.
