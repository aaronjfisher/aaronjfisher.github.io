Aaron Fisher

About

I joined Takeda (Boston, MA) in 2019, as a statistician working on wearable device data and early-stage clinical trials.

Before starting at Takeda, I completed a postdoctoral fellowship with Francesca Dominici (Harvard Biostatistics) and Cynthia Rudin (Duke Computer Science), working on machine learning interpretability, and on causal inference for air pollution studies.

I recieved PhD at the Johns Hopkins Bloomberg School of Public Health Biostatistics department, under the advisement of Vadim Zipunnikov and Brian Caffo. Among other projects there, I worked on fast computations for bootstrapping principal component analysis, with applications in brain MRI data. You can find a software implementation of this work in the bootSVD R package .

Other research interests of mine include functional data analysis, circadian rhythms, Bayesian statistics, adaptive clinical trials, personalized medicine, machine learning, and the perception of statistical graphics.

In my spare time, I like to do walk-runs, slow swims, and kitchen projects that involve lots of test batches.
