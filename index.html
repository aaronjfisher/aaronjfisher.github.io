About

I am a third year PhD candidate in the Biostatistics department at the Johns Hopkins Bloomberg School of Public Health. My thesis research is on high dimensional data decompositions, under the advisement of Vadim Zipunnikov and Brian Caffo. In general, my research interests include high dimensional inference, functional data analysis, neuroimaging analysis, adaptive clinical trials, and the design of intuitive graphics.

In my spare time, I also enjoy casual bike rides, swing dancing, and cooking related projects that involve lots of test batches.
